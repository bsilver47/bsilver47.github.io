Career Goals

Benjamin Sterling Silver

Having been interacting with software from a place of development and creativity for nearly a decade, I naturally find myself drawn towards certain paths and fields. Some of which, that I will discuss here, include Backend Software Engineering, Mobile Engineering, and Communications.

When it comes to software engineering, I have the utmost respect for those who can make things look pretty, but my focus tends to be on making things work. If this involves full-stack development, I can manage, as demonstrated during my tenure at UVU (please see my resume page and github for some of the details on this topic), but I primarily approach software engineering from a place of trying to best deliver the functionality offered by the desired tool. For me, the elegance of trying to deliver the best, most efficient product through hand-curated data is an art form that, ironically, appeals to me more than making things look appealing. As a result of this mindset, I naturally gravitate towards engineering the backend of nearly any service, but especially in the fields that I am the most passionate about, such as medical technologies, telecommunications, and international relations; as will be discussed later in this page.

I have always been enamored by mobile technologies and their ability to streamline the process of accessing information on a day-to-day basis. From making watchfaces and apps for my smartwatch as a teenager to making a calculator application, of sorts, as a means of taking notes in university physics class, I have enjoyed ensuring that access to information is never restricted to a desktop or library environment. In recent years, I have taken a notable liking to GIS and library applications and websites as a means of accessing copious amounts of information freely from anywhere and, in ideal circumstances, at anytime. If I could have a career built on the idea of helping in these endeavors, as highlighted, I would be quite happy.

As an amateur radio operator or, perhaps, as an extension of the reasons I gave for my fondness of mobile engineering, I am particularly partial to the fields of communcations, telecommunications, and signals; here collectively referred to as communications. A generous portion of my time at BYU was dedicated to focusing on how to adapt a protocol designed for the internet to a true Over-The-Air setting or medium. Part of this was the designated task of attempting to use the protocol with satellite communications, allowing for more free use of communications of nearly all varieties in restricted or remote areas. Another aspect of this was the elected task of attempting to facilitate use of the protocol in lower frequencies, allowing for easier consumer and amateur access from remote and destabalized locations. The intention of both compontents was to aid in the facilitation of more reliable communication - that no singular event could restrict the ability for anyone to access what they need.

As indicated, each of these three fields play into three main industries for me: medical, communications, and international relations. Each of these, and more, are industries I would be particularly hopeful to pursue in my career. To give an idea of what this might look like, I would like to discuss one of my favorite interactions from my time employeed by Cox Communications as a full-time employee. I was in a role that was particularly focused on getting sales; there were other components as well, such as tech support and relations, but theoretically the biggest component of my paycheck was supposed to come from a commission of the sales I made each month. That being said, I gravitated towards more of a niche aspect of this: the Deaf Community. You see, learning to communicate in ASL helped me become a better communicator than I had otherwise been, and communicating with the members of that community came with ancillary benefits such as learning the programs and policies legally allotted to those that needed or could justify need for certain benefits, otherwise not known. As time progressed, I started to form relationships with some of the community, and began to grow in a number of ways that would not have otherwise been afforded to me. In this way, interacting with speakers of other languages, such as ASL, I have learned a lot, beyond that just learning the language, especially in helping people communicate with their loved ones, regardless of circumstances like location. This has been something that I feel very blessed to have done, and would enjoy being able to help in even greater ways in the future, regardless of the capacity; again, the ideal being means being through communications, mobile engineering, backend software engineering, or any of the other fields mentioned.
